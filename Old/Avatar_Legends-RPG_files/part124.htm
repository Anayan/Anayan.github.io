The following pages contain information on these NPCs along with stats you can use for them in game—a principle (when appropriate), combat techniques, conditions, and fatigue are all listed for each character.

NPCs tied to a faction or group that come into play in the adven- ture are listed after the faction itself. In a combat, the gang and the leader would act separately in exchanges and choosing techniques.

Lei

Minor NPC

Rose’s mother is a wealthy diplomat in Ba Sing Se and works on fostering relations between the Fire Nation and Earth Kingdom after the Hundred Year War. In search of her daughter, Lei has gone to the carnival because this was the last place Rose was seen. Obsessed with maintaining the family’s pristine public image, Lei has struggled to keep her headstrong daughter in line and now balances her worry for Rose with her desire to settle the “unfortunate and uncomfort- able circumstances” quietly.


Drive: Find Rose Principle: Self-Control Conditions: Afraid Fatigue:


Rose

MaJor NPC

Rose is a wealthy young woman who is studying to be a philosopher… but who has also fallen in love with a low-class member of the Paper Lantern Gang, Hua. Rose, the daughter of a Fire Nation colonial moth- er and Earth Kingdom diplomat father, grew up in a former Fire Nation colony and has struggled to fit in since her family was called back to Ba Sing Se after the war’s end. Growing up as a half–Fire Nation teenager in an Earth Kingdom stronghold like Ba Sing Se has caused Rose no small amount of grief—she hates the capital and wants to move back to the countryside. Rose and Hua’s relationship is a secret, and they have plans to elope, just as soon as Hua can get out from under Cong’s thumb. Rose has never quite felt like she belonged, and her personality is characterized by her individualistic and defiant nature.


Drive: Escape to the countryside with Hua

Principle: Freedom

Conditions: Angry, Guilty, Troubled

Fatigue:

Techniques: Jet Stepping, Sense Environment

The Flying Koi Performers

MaJor NPC Group

Swathed in a veritable rainbow of cartwheeling colors and patterns, this group of performers loyally follow Ringmaster Peng, who gives young runaways with curious talents—benders and non-benders alike—a place to call home. Each circus worker is unique in some way, whether they express themself in elaborate costume or specialized talent; for instance, an acrobat might walk around on her hands

while a Firebender might create amazing firework displays using his bending. While performers distract circusgoers in the most spectacu- lar fashion, those in the shadows have their hands in guests’ pockets. The performers of the Flying Koi are thieves in disguise, executing a number of increasingly bold thefts and using the money they steal from the rich city nobles to feed the poor in the Lower Ring.


Drive: Take them for all they’re worth

Principle: Justice

Conditions: Afraid, Angry, Guilty

Fatigue:

Technique: Scatter and Regroup

Maolo

MaJor NPC

Maolo is the adopted son of Ringmaster Peng and one of the Flying Koi Carnival’s most talented performers. Acrobatics, animal handling, fire juggling—Maolo does it all, but he’s best known by his stage name “The Paintbender” for how he waterbends the liquid in paint to create fantastic displays onstage. In his clandestine work, he uses his waterbending talents to forge paintings from noble families while the originals are auctioned off at Ba Sing Se’s black market. Maolo is deeply loyal to his father but even more so to all the Flying Koi per-

formers, his chosen family. Maolo yearns for his father’s retirement so that he can take on the ringmaster mantle and run the circus his way.


Drive: Become ringmaster

Principle: Duty

Conditions: Angry, Guilty, Insecure

Fatigue:

Techniques: Flow as Water, Water whip


Ringmaster Peng

Master NPC

Peng, the Flying Koi Carnival’s leader, grew up dirt-poor in the Lower Ring during the Hundred Year War. Years of scraping by from day to day embittered him, and when he created the Flying Koi Carnival, his goal was to change the fortunes of those who have nothing. In front of circus crowds, Ringmaster Peng is a smiling showman, but behind closed doors, everything about Peng changes: his posture, his voice, his facial expressions…While still kind to his performers, Peng is ruthless in protecting the carnival’s secret cause.


Drive: Steal from the rich to give to the poor

Principle: Equality

Conditions: Angry, Afraid, Guilty, Insecure, Troubled

Fatigue:

Techniques: Attack Weakness, Entangler, Smoke Bomb


Anri

MaJor NPC

Anri is an artist and performer at heart who’s dreamt of joining the circus since she was a child. She’s always accompanied by her trained elephant-rat, Lulu. Despite her proximity in age to Maolo, she joined the Flying Koi Carnival as Maolo’s apprentice, using her earthbending skills to manipulate colorful clays in the water he bends. Recently, she graduated to being Maolo’s partner-in-crime, but she hasn’t yet been able to shake her self-image as a doting apprentice. Unlike Maolo, she’s fully sold on Peng’s mission despite how ruthless he’s become… and despite Maolo confiding his worries in her. Anri has some tough decisions to make: does she betray her former master’s confidence for an opportunity to get closer to the Ringmaster?

The Paper Lantern Gang Members

MaJor NPC Group

The Paper Lantern Gang is a violent street gang led by the brutish Cong. Leaning on the intimidating presence of their leader and the ruthless enforcement of Cong’s second-in-command, a Firebender named Hua, the gang has lived comfortably, holding their territory in the Middle Ring in a vice grip—that is, until the Flying Koi Carnival moved in. The thieving carnival refuses to pay the gang what they

owe for working the Paper Lanterns’ territory. Rumor has it the Paper Lantern Gang is the laughingstock of Ba Sing Se’s criminal underbel- ly, and the gang members are getting restless for their leader to do something. The gang’s home base lies beneath a small tea shop called the Stone Kettle.


Drive: Defend our territory and reputation

Principle: Pride

Conditions: Angry, Foolish, Insecure

Fatigue:

Technique: Overwhelm


Cong

Master NPC

Cong is the brutish leader of the Paper Lantern Gang, and his im- pressive earthbending skills and intimidating physical presence terrify most people—his gang included. Despite his martial skills, he’s not the brightest person and relies on his second-in-command, Hua, to help him keep control of his territory. In fact, despite his reputation, he spends much of his time perfecting the art of papercutting and has an extensive collection of handmade shadow puppets. Cong doesn’t tolerate any ridicule of his hobby, though it is sometimes joked about in whispered tones in criminal circles. Away from his papercutting supplies, you’d never guess this violent Earthbender had such a delicate hand.


Drive: Enforce my rule

Principle: Power

Conditions: Angry, Afraid, Guilty, Insecure, Troubled

Fatigue:

Techniques: Earth Sinking, Stone Shield


Drive: Make a name for myself

Principle: Beauty

Conditions: Angry, Guilty, Insecure

Fatigue:

Technique: Rock Column

Hua

Master NPC

Hua is Cong’s second-in-command…but she despises him; when he’s not brutish, crass, and violent, he’s lazy, leaving all the hard work to her. In contrast, Hua is quiet and calm unless circumstances call for her frightening firebending skills; in the Paper Lantern Gang, she’s known for swift and extreme enforcement. Hua and Rose have been secretly in love since they quite literally bumped into one another in the street…as Hua was trying to steal Rose’s purse. Ever since they met, Rose has been helping Hua undermine Cong’s leadership and plotting a way to escape the city.


Drive: Run away with Rose

Principle: Self-Reliance

Conditions: Angry, Afraid, Guilty, Insecure, Troubled

Fatigue:

Techniques: Fire Pinwheel, Flame Knives


Detective Ziming

MaJor NPC

Detective Ziming is a Ba Sing Se native who grew up in a strict family of city guards. Ziming is the youngest of five siblings, and despite being incredibly bright, she’s fully aware that she owes her recent career advancement to her family ties. This makes her even more determined to prove she deserves her detective badge. Ziming has noticed a rise in crime since the Flying Koi Carnival came to town, and though her colleagues dismiss the correlation, Ziming can’t let go of the weird feeling she has that the carnival is to blame… The detective is eager and suspicious, and she’s desperate to solve a case to quell her insecurity, which manifests in nervous habits such as touching her hair, repeating or second-guessing herself, and becom- ing swiftly defensive.


Drive: Prove I earned my badge

Principle: Pride

Conditions: Angry, Guilty, Insecure

Fatigue:

Technique: Chart a Course


Ibuki

Minor NPC

Ibuki is an informant Ziming uses to get information on the various criminal enterprises in the city. He’s sneaky and sly, and he can get into most places without an issue. Plus, he has no qualms about working with a detective for the right price…or betraying a detective, for that matter. Ibuki invests the money he earns in his future, and for him, that means valuable art pieces. He’s a collector of sorts, often skulking about the city’s black markets for choice masterpieces and rare finds.


The Cabbage Merchant

Minor NPC

This unfortunate soul witnessed Rose’s kidnapping with his beloved turkey duck Daisy while picking up a few cabbages that fell to the ground. The kidnapper took Daisy from the farmer as collateral for his silence—as long as the Cabbage Merchant stays silent, his be- loved pet won’t be hurt. However, a young woman’s life could be on the line… What is an honest merchant to do? The Cabbage Merchant is a simple, earnest man who needs help. Aside from the perilous job of selling and protecting his cabbages (you’d be surprised at the sorts of people he’s run into over the years!) the Cabbage Merchant isn’t one for action and excitement. He needs someone to help him out of the pickle he’s in and to save his beloved Daisy!


Drive: Ensure Daisy’s safe return home

Principle: Honesty Conditions: Afraid Fatigue:


Drive: Make the best deal possible

Principle: Survival Conditions: Afraid Fatigue:
