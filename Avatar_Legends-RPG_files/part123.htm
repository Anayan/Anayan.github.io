Escalations

Through the course of this adventure, use an escalation if the conflicts start to falter, the pace of the story slows, or the players are unsure of what to do. Each escalation reveals new information about the situation and forces the PCs into action. If players are invested in Rose’s kidnapping but unsure how to proceed, escala- tions toss clues or key characters into their path. If the players are not invested in Rose’s kidnapping, escalations give them opportu- nities to become involved or, if PCs stay hands-off, to demonstrate how their inaction does or does not affect the city around them. You can read more about crafting your own escalations on page

261. Here are some examples of escalations you can use:

• Cabbages tumble through the streets, chased by a frantic Cabbage Merchant. A witness to the kidnapping, the Cabbage Merchant can be a helpful clue-giver

for stuck investigators if they show him kindness and help him gather the spilled vegetables.

• A violent clash over territory erupts in the streets between the Paper Lanterns and Flying Koi Carnival. If no one stops them, the fight eventually ends but not before causing injuries to innocent bystanders and irreparable damage to surrounding property. During the chaos, a ransom note is slipped into Lei’s pocket, implicating

the winning faction and demanding money…or else.

• As her case comes to a close, Detective Ziming identifies a culprit—either Ringmaster Peng or Gang Leader Cong, depending on the PCs’ location—and leads

a raid on the Flying Koi Carnival or Paper Lantern headquarters. If no one intervenes, the leader is apprehended and led away while loudly declaring they have new information to prove their innocence.
