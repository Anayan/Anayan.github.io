Background on p.78


“I needed to understand what real suffering was, so I could become more compassionate to others.”

Drive

Forge a new path for the future


How to Play Korra

• Say what’s on your mind. Korra speaks her mind to anyone who is willing to listen (and yells it at those who aren’t). Speaking out of turn has embarrassed her more than a few times, but Korra

is honest with her emotions and open in her communication.

• Care with abandon. The Avatar genuinely cares about her role and isn’t apathetic or distant. She’s willing to give her all when solving a situation and expects those around her to do the same.

• Follow your own path. There’s little Korra can’t do if she puts her mind to it and she’s always forged her own path to being the Avatar. Korra acknowledges the past, but believes the best way forward is trying in earnest and doing your best.

Bring Korra in…

• …when a tyrannical foe needs to be taught a lesson.

• …when spirits are in danger.

• …when there is a significant and seemingly insoluble problem.


Techniques

Flame Knives

advance & attack

Mark up to 3-fatigue. Hold an equal number of flames. Lose 1-flame at the end of each exchange after this one. When you inflict fatigue or conditions on a foe, inflict an additional 1-fatigue for each remaining flame.


Hurl Metal*

advance & attack


metal

Rip and bend big pieces of metal in the surrounding area to strike at a foe. Mark 1-fatigue and inflict 2-fatigue or one condition, your

choice, on your target. They also either lose a positive status or suffer

Impaired as they are knocked off-balance.


Refresh

evade & observe


heal

Apply water to reinvigorate and close wounds on a willing target. Mark fatigue to heal an ally in reach who is evading and observing. Clear an appropriate status from them, and clear 3-fatigue or two conditions.
